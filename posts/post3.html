What is Machine Learning?

15 Yanvar, 2026

Computers usually do things that we are given specific instructions for adding, subtracting, storing information. But there are problems in life that cannot be solved by simple rules. For example, determining whether an email is spam or not, distinguishing a cat from a dog in a picture, or detecting diseases at an early stage. It is to solve such problems that the field of Machine Learning was needed.

Machine Learning is a field of artificial intelligence that gives computers the ability to perform tasks by learning from data, instead of being explicitly programmed. In classical programming, a programmer writes each step by hand. In Machine Learning, a model sees thousands of examples and finds patterns on its own. Just like a child learns to distinguish "this is a dog, this is a cat" after hearing it many times.

The history of Machine Learning dates back to the mid-20th century. The initial ideas came from research in statistics and artificial intelligence. In the 1950s, Alan Turing posed the question, "Can a machine think?" Later, neural networks, decision trees, and statistical models were developed. The real turning point came in the 2000s, when large amounts of data and powerful computers appeared, it was from that moment that Machine Learning began to produce practical results.

Today, there are several main directions of Machine Learning. In Supervised Learning, a model learns from specified examples, for example, I used this method in my Titanic project to predict the survival of passengers. In Unsupervised Learning, a model finds groups on its own from unspecified data. In Reinforcement Learning, a model learns through interaction with the environment, using a system of rewards and punishments.

Machine Learning is now embedded in many parts of our lives. Facial recognition on your phone, YouTube recommendations, early detection of diseases in medicine, increased productivity in agriculture - all these are practical results of ML. Most importantly, this field is still in its infancy - new discoveries and new methods are emerging every year. Therefore, studying ML is a very valuable investment not only for the present, but also for the future.
